# Sodra Visual Review

## Initial comparison

- The previous Sodra model had the basic curved-front idea, but it did not carry the reference building's office-headquarters silhouette.
- The dark curved roof read as a detached crescent instead of a supported overhang sitting on the facade.
- The upper pavilion was a simple glass box on isolated posts, with weak connection to the roof structure below.
- The facade fins were sparse bars placed on the outside of the building rather than a repeated structural curtain-wall system.
- Glass areas were too generic and continuous; the reference has stronger white bands, mullions, and office-window rhythm.
- The side wings and white core did not read as multiple connected building masses.
- The lawn/site area was a flat rectangular pad with little grounding logic.

## Pass goals

- Pass 1: rebuild the main curved body, connect side wings, make roof and pavilion physically supported.
- Pass 2: refine facade rhythm with repeated fins, mullions, bands, clearer glazing and Sodra sign placement.
- Pass 3: polish proportions, grounding, preview lighting and hero-camera readability.
