# Visma Visual Review

## Initial comparison

- The previous Visma model had the correct white-wing/orange-center concept, but the three main masses read as separated blocks rather than one corporate office building.
- The entrance was attached as a small front object, not integrated into a central glazed atrium.
- Roof pieces were split into separate caps and did not form the flatter continuous roofline visible in the reference.
- Window placement was generic and sparse; the reference has a calmer office rhythm with larger central glazing and consistent side-wing grids.
- The stairs and forecourt existed, but the landing/door/canopy relationship was still too blockout-like.
- The site trees and paving helped scale but did not compensate for the disconnected building massing.

## Pass goals

- Pass 1: unify the three building volumes, create a continuous roofline, and connect entrance/steps to the main structure.
- Pass 2: refine the central glass atrium, orange facade rhythm, white wing grids, canopy, and Visma sign.
- Pass 3: polish proportions, materials, grounding, preview lighting and hero-camera readability.
